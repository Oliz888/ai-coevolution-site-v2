v1 → v2.1 Expansion

The original corpus (110 papers) was expanded with 137 papers from OpenAlex systematic search, adding new characters and works not found in v1.

Interpretation: The corpus is not yet saturated. The last 20 papers contribute new characters at 2.0× the rate of the first 20, and new works at 1.0×. Q3 and Q4 together add 388 new characters and 211 new works. However, the core canonical figures are stable — the top 20 anchor characters have not changed since v1. The long tail of peripheral characters continues to grow, which is expected for an interdisciplinary corpus.

Backward Citation Coverage

As a complementary validation, we extracted reference lists from 82 corpus papers (4,504 individual references) and cross-matched them against the full 247-paper corpus. This tests whether the papers our corpus cites are themselves included — a standard bibliometric completeness check.

78.1%Internal cross-citation
193Papers cited by ≥1 other
70Cited by ≥3 papers
8Genuine Tier-1 gaps

78.1% of corpus papers (193 / 247) appear in at least one other corpus paper's reference list, indicating strong internal cohesion. Only 8 genuine academic gaps were found at the core topic level (sci-fi + robots/AI + society), and none are foundational works. The top 2 gaps by citation count:

PaperCited byTopic
Sartori & Bocca (2023)3 corpus papersPublic perceptions of AI and sociotechnical imaginaries
Cave et al. (2018)2 corpus papersPortrayals and perceptions of AI and why they matter

The remaining 6 gaps are each cited by only 1 paper, suggesting they are peripheral rather than core. No foundational works (Jasanoff & Kim 2015, Borup et al. 2006, etc.) are missing. Most unmatched in-scope references are primary sources (novels, films), blog posts, or news articles — not academic papers that belong in the corpus.
